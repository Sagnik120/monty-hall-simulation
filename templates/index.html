How the Monty Hall Problem Works

The simulation follows these steps:

  1. The car is randomly placed behind one of the three doors (the others have goats)
  2. The contestant randomly selects one door (this simulation does this automatically)
  3. The host (who knows what's behind the doors) opens a different door that has a goat
  4. Depending on the selected strategy:
    • Stay: The contestant keeps their original choice
    • Switch: The contestant switches to the other unopened door
  5. The outcome is recorded and visualized in the chart

Why Switching is Better

When you first choose a door, you have a 1/3 chance of picking the car and a 2/3 chance of picking a goat.

If you initially picked a goat (which happens 2/3 of the time), the host will reveal the other goat, and switching will win you the car.

If you initially picked the car (1/3 of the time), switching will lead to a goat.

Therefore, switching gives you a 2/3 chance of winning, while staying with your original choice gives only a 1/3 chance.
